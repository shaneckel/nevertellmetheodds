(FULL-TIME POSITION)

STATUS: NON-EXEMPT

SUPERVISOR: DIRECTOR OF JFCS COMMUNITY FOOD PANTRY

QUALIFICATIONS:
Bachelors in Social Work, licensed in the State of Pennsylvania (LSW or LCSW). A minimum of 1-3 years’ experience providing case management services for multi-problem households and domestic abuse is preferred. Must be able to work with a variety of social service systems. Demonstrated interpersonal, organizational, and communication skills. Ability to work both independently and collaboratively. Sensitive to cultural differences of clients and willingness to learn about laws, customs and traditions of the Orthodox Jewish community.

POSITION SUMMARY:
The Specialist is responsible for assisting JFCS Community Food Pantry clients. The Specialist will help Orthodox Jewish and other pantry clients facing multiple barriers to self-sufficiency access available services and identify solutions to overcome challenges. The Specialist will also be attuned to the particular needs of households experiencing domestic violence. While the Specialist will have particular responsibility for supporting Orthodox Jewish clients, their role would not be exclusive to this population.

DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES:
1. Provide outreach to the Orthodox Jewish community, developing relationships with key community and religious leaders;
2. Participate in training and development activities including those which would result in increased responsiveness to Orthodox Jewish clients’ needs throughout the social service sector;
3. Supervise pantry and orient program volunteers to Orthodox Jewish cultural practices as appropriate;
4. Identify families with multiple needs through outreach efforts, telephone interviewing and home visits;
5. Determine eligibility, complete intake and assessment for all new clients for JFCS Community Food Pantry;
6. Respond to the needs of families through the provision of coordination, referral, and support services;
7. Support Orthodox families experiencing domestic violence and child abuse by creating linkages with DV & CYF caseworkers with specialized knowledge of the Jewish community;
8. Develop individual and family plans of action;
9. Document progress of clients toward addressing needs;
10. Meet regularly with clients in office, home and community settings to address needs and progress;
11. Link clients to appropriate services at JFCS and other service providers;
12. Work with other service providers to ensure all necessary services are being provided;
13. Attend inter-agency meetings on the clients’ behalf;
14. Maintain up-to-date computer and paper clients’ records to document needs, progress, and outcomes;
15. Assist in development of program outcomes and other measures;
16. Provide coverage of pantry services and food distribution efforts as needed;
17. Complete other duties as assigned by supervisor.

The primary objective of the Production Design and Fabrication Manager is to oversee the production processes and design of all fabrication and design projects, schedule assets, align the team with project goals and provide insight into best practices to meet and exceed those goals. The Production Design and Fabrication Manager must have strong knowledge fabrication techniques, skills in leadership, scheduling multiple projects and prioritization.

Who you are

You know your way around the tool shed. You have prior experience coordinating resources and schedules for a fabrication shop, design studio or space program. You are exacting, punctual, analytical and meticulous. Having mastered the art of the Gantt chart, you can wrangle dependencies, octopuses, cats and creative types with ease. You've got exceptional organizational skills, bordering on obsession, but you’re not afraid to get your hands dirty. When you roll up, resource management is in the driver seat and quality control is riding shotgun. (It is pretty much a given that you probably designed and built your ride as well.) Inflexible deadlines are familiar to you, as are contingency plans for your contingency plans.

Requirements:

The applicant must have the ability to lead our fabrication team and maintain multiple concurrent project schedules using gantt charts and production calendars.
The applicant must be able to see the “big picture” and have the long-term foresight to navigate around potential production roadblocks. When issues with schedules arise we need someone who can react fluidly to help get projects completed on time.
The applicant must be proactive and solution oriented.
The applicant must have the ability to communicate effectively with a multi-disciplinary project team of fabricators and designers.
The applicant must possess experience and knowledge of the tools, materials, and techniques typical to wood substrate, plastic, and metal fabrication.


Bonus points:

Familiarity with 3D modeling software such as fusion 360, or Rhinoceros 3D.
Familiarity with CNC machines (router, mill, laser cutter, 3D printers).
Hidden talents to help our team grow.


Responsibilities:

Keep all aspects of fabrication on schedule and ensure deliverables are ready on time.
Track all schedule changes and document them so the team stays on target.
Assist in the coordination of project teams and development of best practices.
Advise in fabrication processes and techniques.
Ensure the production team has everything they need to be successful and efficient.
Source 3rd party vendors and contractors as needed.
Host retrospective meetings after project completion to talk about what worked and what we can strive to improve in the future.


Who we are

Iontank is a specialized design studio that develops interactive art installations and software systems for Fortune 100 companies, award-winning ad agencies, world-renowned museums, industry-leading exhibition firms and inspiring event producers.
When physical meets digital, we rock. We have an extensive collection of fabrication tools and warehouses full of amazing tech toys at our disposal to help you deliver your most ambitious project to date.

We love our craft. Our crew has a complete mastery of pixels, code, circuits, CNC machines, lasers and soldering irons. We are disciplined craftsmen, coders, designers, techs and artists that happen to be fluent in logistics, can easily read blueprints upside down, understand electrical loads and get the details right the first time. Every time.

Since 2001 we have been helping clients to blow minds and stand apart.
